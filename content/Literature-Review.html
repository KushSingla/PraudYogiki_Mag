What is a literature review ?

  • A literature review is a body of text having critical points of the current research knowledge, including substantive findings as well as theoretical or methodological knowledge on a particular point.
  • It usaually precedes the research proposals and reserach results.
  • The ultimate goal of a literature review: is to provide the user with current knowledge on a particular topic, that makes the basis of a research. Also helps to work on the future topics.
  • Limit the problem area
  • define the problem
  • Avoid unnecessary repetition
  • Search for new approaches
  • Sample the current knowledge
  • Recommend suitable methods

Importance of literature review

  • Identify and remove the bias which may be introduced into specific studies by their authors
  • Define and delineate the problem area in relation to the existing studies
  • Compare methodologies employed in existing studies
  • Utilize data collection or other instruments which may already be developed
  • Check results obtained by other researchers
  • Replicate an existing study in a new context

Sources of information

Primary sources
# Author himself reads about the reserach first hand
# Original research work published in journals, research reports, collective works and conference papers.
# Primary sources allow the researchers to get as close as possible to the original research ideas, events, and empirical research as possible.
# Such sources includes: creative works, first hand or contemporary accounts of events, the publication of the results of empirical observations or research.
# These sources refer to the first place where a researcher will reveal to the scientific community in a publically accessible document of the results of scientific knowledge. In many cases, the document is rigorously reviewd by peers in research community to maintain integrity of the scientific knowledge.
Secondary sources
# These sources analyze and talk about the information from other sources, mainly primary sources.
# These sources organize the primary literature and make retrival of items of interest much easier. Author is not directly involved in the research as a researcher, observer or participant.
# Others that come under secondary sources are: text books; handbooks; abstracts; research reviews; journal articles; abstracts services; scopus: review articles; biographies; history or historical content; political analysis; essays on scientific contents; monographs; reference works; current awareness tools; trade catalogues.

Organization of literature review

A general organization of literature review looks like a funnel

  • Broader topics
  • Subtopics
  • studies like yours

how to organize studies

  • Chronological
    • by publication date
    • by trend
  • thematic
    • A structure which considers different themes
  • Methodological
    • Focuses on the methods of the researcher, e.g., qualitative versus quantitative approaches.

Writing the text of the review of literature

  • write title of the paper from journal
  • its author(s)
  • year, volume & place of publication
  • page nos.
  • purpose, methodology, sample, tools used
  • Findings and scope of future research etc.

Referencing

  • provide full details of all the sources cited in the dissertation
  • should include published books or articles, book chapters, technical reports, web sources, etc.
  • list alphabetically by author name (name of the first author is the case of works with co-authors)
  • make sure you understand the university regulations on plagiarism
  • consult your department guidelines for more on referencing style.

Rules of referencing

  • Begin references on a new page with the title references centered at the top.
  • Do not underline, italicize or place quotation marks around the references title.
  • your references should be alphabetized by the last names of the first author of each source.
  • all references should be double-spaced.
  • each reference use a handing indentation: the first line of the reference should be flush left, but each additional line of the reference needs to be indended
  • In article titles, only the first letter should be capitalized. If a colon appears in the title, the first letter after the colon should also be capitalized. The title should not be placed in quotations, underlined or italicized.
  • All major words in the title of a journal should be capitalized; i.e. The Journal of Personality and Social Psychology.
  • Longer words such as books and journals should appear in italics.
