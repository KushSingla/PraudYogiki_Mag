Steps of research methodology

In general it means steps that are to be taken to do reserach. There are four steps:

Selecting and defining a research problem
A review of related literature is done to specify the research problem, research questions and hypotheses.
Describing the research methodology
Selecting an approporiate research design.
Collecting of data
Using instruments, recording data, scoring and tabulating the former and later for analysis.
Analyze and interpret the results
Selecting and appying appropriate statistical tools to arrive to valid conclusions.

Anyways, today we are starting with "Hypothesis". As the name suggests, I think most of you know about what hypothesis means. To be formal, A hypothesis is a guess or attempt to explain the nature of relationship between variables indentified in the problem.

Hypothesis are very common, apart from research problem hypothesis are made daily to any kind of problem. In simpler terms, a hypothesis is a simple effort made to solve a problem by assuming different situations or connections between the variables of the problem.

Some points related to hypothesis:

  • Hypotheses flow from the problem statement, literature review, and the theroretical framework.
  • Hypotheses are formulated before the study is conducted because they provide direction for the collection, analysis and intrepretation of data.

So after understanding the definition of hypothesis lets move forward towards the functions which a hypothesis performs.

The functions are::

  • It gives a statement of a relationship that is empirically testable and can be found to be either true or false.
  • It gives direction to a study.(i.e. design, sampling, data collection .. etc.)
  • It suggests the type of statistical analysis to be used in the study.
  • It gives continuity to a study by providing a basis for drawing conclusions from the study.

Moving forward, we have the types of hypotheses:

  • Null hypothesis: It is a statement of the status quo, one of no difference or no effect. If the null hypothesis is not rejected, no changes will be made.
  • Alternative hypothesis: It is one in which some difference or effect is expected. Accepting the alternative hypothesis will lead to changes in opinions or actions.
  • Directional hypothesis: It specifies the expected direction of the relationship between independent and dependent variables.
  • Non-directional hypothesis: It indicates the existence of a relationship between the variables but does not specify the anticipated (predicted/expected) direction of the relationship.

Now after listening all the definitions given above we conclude that:

  • One researcher need to conver the research question to null and alternative hypotheses.
  • The null hypothesis (H0) is a claim of "no difference in the population" (it states that there is no relationship betweeen independent and dependent variables)
  • The alternative hypothesis (Ha) claims "H0 is false" (it states that it is a statement of expected relationship between the variables. It can be directional or non-directional)
  • In a summary one can also say that collecting data and seeking evidence against H0 as a way of bolstering (supporting) Ha (deduction).

When we have learned all about what hypothesis is and what are the functions of a hypothesis then we need to understand what we conclude from the hypothesis or we can say what conclusions we have formed.

So forming conclusions have some key points in case of hypothesis as follows:

  • every hypothesis test ends with the experimentations either:
    • rejecting the null hypothesis
    • failing to reject the null hypothesis
  • null hypothesis is something that is defined but it's never accepted. The reason is every time a researcher will definitely have some changes which need to be done in the initial hypothesis (which means rejection of null hypothesis). But, this state can come where one has to accept null hypothesis when researcher doesn't have enough evidence to support his change or any alteration in the initial hypothesis.

Before proceeding towards the ending there are some famous quotes which are related to hypothesis that are:

  • A hypothesis can either be supported or not supported by study findings.
  • Rejection of the statistical hypothesis is equivalant to acceptance of research hypothesis.

  • Does the hypothesis directly relate to the research problem ?

  • Has the hypothesis been developed prior to collecting the data ?
  • is the hypothesis testable ? (define variables & indicate their relationships/measurements)
